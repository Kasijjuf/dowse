Summary

tl;dr: Dowse is free software dedicated to enhancing the conviviality of your online networking experience, and your peace of mind. Preserving your privacy is part of this mission. Dowse will never spy on you nor disclose your network information to anyone.

^^ #privacy-by-design

Privacy by Design

Dyne:dowse is free software. It follows the principle of privacy by design: that means the developers care about the your freedom, and protecting your privacy.

The objective of Dowse is to put you in control of your own network, and protect your privacy from prying eyes, from advertisers on the Web, to your ISP, or rogue devices on your local network.

Dowse does not, and will never provide any information on your local network to third parties, including to Dowse developers and partners. Your network is yours, and your privacy is critical to the success of our products. There is not, however, any security measure that will prevent a determined individual from voluntarily leaking information from within your network to a third party. Our promise is that if such an attempt is detected, Dowse will tell you, and if there is a way to prevent it, Dowse will implement it in the best of our developers' abilities.

^^ #network-monitoring

Network Monitoring

In order to manage devices and provide safe networking conditions on your network, Dowse is monitoring connecting devices, and make them visible to the administrators of the Dowse appliance.

Identifying information such as the MAC address, and other device information is used by Dowse to provide services, such as selective connectivity to unknown devices, or plain rejection of unwanted devices. This information is stored on the Dowse appliance.

No information about your network needs to be sent beyond it, so Dowse is not sending anything about it outside of your local network.

^^ #data-storage

Data Storage

Privacy-sensitive data is stored on the Dowse device. The storage is NOT YET made on encrypted filesystem, so we recommend that you encrypt your filesystem prior to installing Dowse software.

Dowse keeps information about known devices, but never communicates that data to anyone but you. When it is implemented, you can use the RESET button to empty the database and restart the Dowse system. It will recreate its database and populate it with the devices it detects when it starts.

^^ #remote-access

Remote Access

You can choose to enable upgrade detection: Dowse will connect to the dowse.equipment service over Tor to retrieve information about an eventual new release. You should always use your operating system's package manager to perform upgrades. Dowse does not provide automated upgrades besides that of the operating system.

The Dowse appliance is accessible via this Web interface, but only from the local network. Privacy-sensitive operations require administrative control, which can be setup using the pairing mechanism upon installation. Once this step has been performed, further devices can be authorized by existing administrators.

All Dowse configuration, including accessible via this Web interface, are done via the Dowse API. All operations require authentication and authorization from known local clients.

Dowse does not offer any access from outside the LAN.

Some modules might provide extra services, e.g., to enable a Tor relay, or an SSH tunnel: use them with care. Such modules are labeled appropriately.

^^ #policy-update

Privacy Policy Updates

Policy updates will happen via release upgrades. They will not change to rip you off your privacy, because we're not a corporation, we're not a government, we're not looking to be bought by a corporation, and you're not for sale. Dowse is a free software project dedicated to protect your privacy, and this point will never change.

^^ #canary

Canary: Dowse developers were not contacted by any secret services, or prying systems that may be able to legally silence us. We were not told to introduce a backdoor in our software, track our users, nor do anything susceptible to affect you. We're clear from evil.
